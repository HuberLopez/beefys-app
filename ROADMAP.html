What's in this folder is a prototype — it looks and clicks exactly like the real app, but everything happens inside the visitor's browser. Put it online

Goal: a real link, like beefys.netlify.app, that opens on any phone.  → Click-by-click guide for this phase

  1. Make a free GitHub account

    Go to github.com and sign up. GitHub is free online storage for your app's files. No credit card.

  2. Upload the beefys-app folder

    Create a new repository ("New" → name it beefys-app). On the repo page click "uploading an existing file" and drag in everything from this folder. Done — your files now live online.

  3. Connect it to Netlify

    Go to netlify.com, sign up with your GitHub account, click "Add new site → Import from GitHub", pick beefys-app, and hit Deploy. In ~30 seconds you get a live URL. (Vercel works the same way if you prefer.)

  4. Test the link on your real phone

    Open the URL on your actual phone, not just the computer. Click through Cliente → Operador → Backend. This is your app, live on the internet.

  5. Optional: buy your domain

    Want pedidos.comebeefys.com instead of a netlify.app link? Buy the domain (~$10–15/year at Namecheap) and point it at Netlify — Netlify has a one-page guide under "Domain settings".

⚠︎
One thing to know: the small interface icons load from the internet (a service called Lucide). Once your site is hosted this is totally fine — just know the app needs an internet connection to look perfect.

Make orders actually arrive

Goal: a real order becomes a real WhatsApp message in your phone. This is a legitimate launch.

Your prototype already ends the customer flow with "enviar por WhatsApp" and a payment-proof (comprobante) upload. That is genuinely how many ghost kitchens run — no expensive backend required. Lean into it.

  1. Set up WhatsApp Business

    Download the free WhatsApp Business app and register your kitchen's order number. Free.

  2. Wire the "send" button to your real number

    The button should open WhatsApp with the order + total pre-typed, sent to your number (a wa.me link). This is a small code change — I can do this for you; just give me the number.

  3. Receive → verify → cook

    A real order is now a WhatsApp message with the items, total, and the payment screenshot. You eyeball the comprobante to confirm payment, then cook. The Operador panel becomes your manual checklist for the shift.

  4. Put the link everywhere

    Add your live URL to the @comebeefys Instagram bio and stories. That's your storefront.

Grow into it when volume grows · $0–few hundred

A real backend

Goal: orders saved automatically, the menu editor actually sticks, the kitchen panel updates by itself.

When WhatsApp-by-hand gets busy, this is the upgrade. It's where the app becomes real "software" — orders in a database, a login so only staff see the panels, and the Operador screen lighting up the instant a customer hits send.

  1. Pick a beginner-friendly backend

    Use a "backend-as-a-service" with a generous free tier — Supabase or Firebase. It gives you a database + login without running your own servers.

  2. Store your menu and orders there

    The Backend panel writes the menu to the database; the Cliente app reads it. Now menu edits are permanent and a submitted order is saved, not lost.

  3. Make the Operador panel live

    Turn on "real-time" so a new order pops onto the kitchen screen the moment a customer submits — no refresh, no WhatsApp copy-paste.

  4. Add a simple staff login

    Protect the Operador and Backend panels so only you and your team can open them.

  5. Decide: learn it or hire it

    You can learn this with Supabase's tutorials, or hand a developer this exact prototype as the spec — it already shows every screen and flow, which saves them (and you) real money. Budget a few hundred dollars for a first version.

4
Grow into it optional · varies

Payments

Goal: less manual checking of payment proofs — only when the manual way starts hurting.

  1. Stay manual at first — on purpose

    Pago Móvil / transfer + comprobante screenshot, checked by a human, is valid, free, and what your flow already does. Do not over-engineer payments early.

  2. Automate later if it's worth it

    When volume justifies it, look at a payment gateway to confirm transfers automatically. Keep it simple; for many small kitchens manual Pago Móvil confirmation stays the practical choice.

Before you announce ½ day

Launch checklist

Goal: nothing embarrassing happens on opening day.

  1. Real phone, full run

    Place a complete test order on a real phone — ideally have a friend do it cold and watch where they get confused.

  2. Real content

    Load your actual menu, real prices, correct BCV rate handling, and real delivery zones. No placeholder items.

  3. Confirm orders reach you

    Make sure a submitted order lands in WhatsApp (Phase 2) or the Operador panel (Phase 3) every single time.

  4. Add basic analytics

    Drop in a free tool so you can see how many people visit and where they drop off.

  5. Announce

    Post the link in your @comebeefys stories and pin it to the bio. You're open.
